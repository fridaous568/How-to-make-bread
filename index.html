How to make bread

Agege Bread

Description

Making bread is one of those simple pleasures that fills a house with the best scent known to man most especially in Nigeria. It's mostly about patience giving the yeast time to do its thing while you take the credit.

Recipe details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes (plus 2 hours rising time)
  • Cook Time: 30-35 minutes
  • Servings: 1 large loaf (approx. 12 slices)
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredient

  • Bread Flour : 500g (High-protein flour gives the best chew)
  • Yeast: 7g (1 standard sachet)
  • Salt: 10g (Fine sea salt works best)
  • Warm Water: 325ml (Think lukewarm/baby bath temperature)
  • Unsalted Butter: 30g (Softened)
  • Sugar or Honey: 1 tbsp (To feed the yeast and help browning)

Instruction

  1. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Pour in the warm water and add the softened butter. Mix with your hands or a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.

  2. Knead: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Knead for about 10 minutes by stretching the dough away from you and folding it back. You're done when the dough is smooth, elastic, and springs back when poked.

  3. First Rise: Lightly oil a bowl, place the dough inside, and cover it with a damp cloth. Leave it in a warm, draft-free spot for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  4. Shape: Gently punch the air out of the dough. Shape it into an oval or rectangle to fit a greased loaf tin. Cover again and let it rise for another 45 minutes (the "second prove").

  5. Bake: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Bake for 30-35 minutes. The bread is ready when the crust is golden brown and the bottom sounds hollow when tapped. Let it cool on a wire rack before slicing.

Tips & Notes

  • The Tap Test: Always tap the bottom of the loaf. If it sounds like a drum, it's cooked through. If it's a dull thud, give it 5 more minutes.

  • Steam is Key: a crispier crust, place a small tray of water at the bottom of the oven while it preheats. The steam prevents the crust from setting too quickly, allowing the bread to rise more.

  • Storage: Since this doesn't have preservatives, it's best eaten within 2-3 days. If it goes stale, it makes incredible French toast!
